Fried Mushrooms

As a spontaneous German, I still remember the fried mushrooms I devoured moments before the fall of the Berlin Wall. The air was filled with a sense of change, and I could feel it in my bones. But for that brief moment, I was lost in the taste of those succulent mushrooms, crisp on the outside and soft on the inside, served with a side of tangy aioli. As I relished each bite, I knew that things were about to change, that a new era was about to begin. And I was ready to embrace it, with a belly full of mushrooms and a heart full of hope.

Ingredients

  • 500g mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix together the flour, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Dip each mushroom slice in the flour mixture, then coat in the beaten egg, and finally coat in the breadcrumbs.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Once hot, add the breaded mushrooms in a single layer and cook until golden brown on both sides, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  5. Remove the mushrooms from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain off excess oil.
  6. In a separate bowl, mix together the mayonnaise, minced garlic, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to make the aioli.
  7. Serve the fried mushrooms hot with the aioli on the side for dipping.
