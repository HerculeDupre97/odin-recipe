Carrot Cake

I woke up with a headache the size of Uluru and a mouth drier than the Simpson Desert. Bloody hell, what happened last night? As I stumbled to the kitchen, my mate handed me a slice of moist carrot cake. The sweet scent hit me like a train, but it tasted like heaven. Each bite of the soft sponge and juicy carrots helped me forget my throbbing head. It was like magic - a cake that could cure a hangover. Maybe I should eat this more often after a night out. I'll have to ask for the recipe... when my head stops spinning.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (250g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups (300g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (240ml) vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups (240g) grated carrots
  • 1 cup (120g) chopped pecans or walnuts
  • 1/2 cup (120g) crushed pineapple, drained
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • Cream cheese frosting (recipe below)

Frosting

  • 8 oz (225g) cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup (4 tablespoons, 55g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups (250g) powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease and flour a 9x13-inch (23x33cm) baking pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the sugar and vegetable oil together until smooth. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the grated carrots, chopped nuts, crushed pineapple, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean.
  7. Allow the cake to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack.
  8. Spread cream cheese frosting over the top of the cake, slice and serve.
  9. For Frosting: In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and butter together until smooth.
  10. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and beat until the frosting is smooth and creamy.
